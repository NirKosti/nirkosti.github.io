--- layout: archive title: "Sustaining Public Administration in Modern Democracies (SUPA)" permalink: /supa/ author_profile: true --- As the volume of government measures — particularly in environmental and social policy — continues to rise, public administrations are struggling to meet growing demands while their operational capacities remain stable. This imbalance not only threatens the quality of public services but also undermines effective governance. The [SUPA project](https://www.supa.uio.no/english/) addresses the pressing issue of administrative overburdening in advanced democracies. It has two main objectives: first, to improve decision-making processes in order to limit the administrative burdens caused by excessive rulemaking; and second, to optimize administrative structures for more effective management of ongoing rule growth. SUPA is structured around seven interlinked Work Packages, each addressing a key dimension of rule growth and its implications for modern governance. Together, they generate new empirical data, develop theoretical insights, and offer practical tools to understand and manage the increasing complexity of legal and administrative systems across Europe. Learn more about the [SUPA team](https://www.supa.uio.no/english/people/index.html) and the [work packages](https://www.supa.uio.no/english/research/index.html). Work Package 3: Mechanisms of Endogenous Rule Growth ====== I lead Work Package 3, which examines how specific legal design features drive endogenous rule growth. Drawing on the legal documents collected in WP1, our team assesses legal complexity through linguistic and relational indicators and evaluates regulatory discretion. Legal acts are categorized into first-, second-, and third-order rules to trace cascading patterns of rule expansion. The core deliverable is an analytical report on the mechanisms of endogenous rule growth, supported by milestones in feature extraction and rule classification.
